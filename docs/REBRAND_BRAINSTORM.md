# Rebrand Brainstorm

## Why Rebrand

The current name, `nostr-dm-agent`, over-anchors the product to one interface and one transport layer.

That undersells what the project is becoming:

- A web-first control panel for AI-powered automation
- A hostable core platform with optional micro-app plugins
- A system that can use chat, web, DMs, and later push notifications as interfaces
- A bitcoin-native product with paid setup and hosting potential

The new brand should feel bigger than "DM bot" while still leaving room for Nostr, AI, plugins, payments, and hosting.

## What The Product Really Is

Working definition:

> A self-hostable AI operations platform with installable micro-apps, multi-channel interaction, and bitcoin-native deployment.

In simpler marketing language:

> Run your own AI-powered personal operating system on your server, install apps into it, and control it from web, chat, or Nostr.

## Product Pillars

The brand should reflect these pillars:

1. Web-first experience
2. Multi-channel interaction: web, terminal chat, Nostr DMs, later push
3. Plugin-based micro apps with their own logic and database
4. Shared core services: AI, messaging, Q&A, jobs, files, bookmarks, todos
5. Bitcoin-native monetization and setup
6. Self-hosted, user-owned, extensible system

## Brand Positioning Options

### Option A: AI Operating System

Frame it as a personal or team AI operating system.

Pros:

- Broad and ambitious
- Fits plugins, widgets, commands, and services
- Good for web-first storytelling

Risk:

- "Operating system" is a crowded metaphor

Example line:

> The self-hosted AI operating system for apps, automation, and messaging.

### Option B: App Server For AI Micro-Apps

Frame it as a hostable app platform.

Pros:

- Very aligned with plugins and installable micro-apps
- Easier for technical users to understand
- Good foundation for marketplace language

Risk:

- Slightly less emotionally distinctive

Example line:

> A self-hosted AI app server with installable micro-apps and multi-channel control.

### Option C: Personal Agent Hub

Frame it as a hub where agents, tools, and apps live.

Pros:

- Friendly and understandable
- Good for consumer-facing marketing
- Works for web, chat, notifications

Risk:

- Less differentiated if overused

Example line:

> Your personal AI hub for tasks, files, jobs, bookmarks, and custom apps.

## Suggested Naming Strategy

Use a two-part system:

- Product name: short, memorable, broad
- Descriptor: explains what it is

Examples:

- `Helm` - Self-hosted AI app platform
- `PromptCore` - AI micro-app server for web, chat, and bitcoin-native automation
- `AgentHub` - Installable AI workspace with apps, messaging, and hosting

This gives you room to evolve the descriptor without renaming the product again.

## Ideal Audience

Primary:

- Technical users who want a self-hosted AI control panel
- Nostr-native users who like ownership and open systems
- Indie hackers who want installable AI tools on their server

Secondary:

- Plugin authors who want to publish micro-apps
- Bitcoin users who want sats-native software and hosting
- Small teams who want lightweight internal AI workflows

## Value Proposition

Why someone picks this over a normal chatbot:

- It is not just a chat window
- It is not tied to one UI surface
- It is self-hosted and extensible
- It supports installable apps, not just prompts
- It can become a small platform and marketplace

## Logo Direction

The logo should avoid looking like a generic robot or chat bubble.

Better visual metaphors:

- Helm or steering mark: control, navigation, orchestration
- Modular grid: installable apps and widgets
- Core with orbiting modules: central services plus plugins
- Relay signal + dashboard frame: multi-channel communication
- Bitcoin spark accent: optional secondary brand element, not the whole logo

Style direction:

- Clean geometric mark
- Works as app icon and favicon
- Strong in monochrome first
- Distinct silhouette
- Slightly infrastructural, not playful toy SaaS

Avoid:

- Literal DM icons
- Obvious robot heads
- Overly cyberpunk Bitcoin imagery
- Anything that reads "only Nostr"

## Positioning You Should Probably De-Emphasize

These can still exist in docs, but probably should not lead the brand anymore:

- "DM bot"
- Nostr-first framing
- Phone remote-control novelty as the main story
- Backend implementation details too early

Those are features now, not the product definition.

## Narrative Shift

Old story:

> Control an AI coding bot over Nostr DMs.

New story:

> Run a self-hosted AI platform with installable micro-apps, a web interface, multi-channel control, and bitcoin-native deployment.

## Business Model Angles

You can market multiple revenue layers:

1. Free self-hosted core
2. Paid one-click install scripts
3. Paid managed hosting
4. Official premium plugins
5. Plugin marketplace revenue share later

## Recommended Immediate Next Moves

1. Pick a new umbrella name that does not mention DMs or Nostr.
2. Rewrite the README headline and first paragraph around web-first AI platform language.
3. Create a simple landing page with hero, official plugins, multi-channel support, and hosting story.
4. Standardize the product vocabulary: `core`, `apps`, `plugins`, `widgets`, `channels`, `hosting`.
5. Write a short plugin author pitch page.
6. Separate "Nostr support" from the main identity and present it as one powerful interface among several.

## My Recommendation

If you want the safest strong move:

- Brand direction: web-first self-hosted AI app platform
- Naming direction: short platform name plus descriptive subtitle
- Best candidates to test first: `Helm`, `PromptCore`, `AgentHub`

If I had to turn this into a first marketing draft today, I would position it like this:

> `Helm` is a self-hosted AI app platform. Install micro-apps like todos, bookmarks, jobs, and files. Control them from the web, terminal, or Nostr. Extend the platform with plugins. Deploy with bitcoin-native setup or hosted options.

## Brainstorm Questions

1. Do you want the brand to feel more technical infrastructure or more productized consumer tool?
2. Should bitcoin be a primary identity element or a secondary differentiator?
3. Do you want Nostr visible on the homepage, or mostly in docs and advanced features?
4. Do you want plugin authors to be a core audience from day one, or a later growth loop?
5. Do you want the name to sound more open-source/devtool or more polished SaaS?
